Dead Horse State Park

Do you want to skip the big crowds that national parks like Arches and Canyonlands draw on holiday weekends, but still enjoy the natural beauty of Utah? Book a campsite at Dead Horse State Park and get both. Located just about a half hour from Arches, Dead Horse State Park boasts red rock canyons, high desert vistas, thrilling outdoor adventures and a rustic camping experience. The park campground has standard partial hookup sites for RVs, tent camping spots and yurt rentals. There are no showers at the park and the closest services are 35 miles away in Moab.

Rocks State Park

Known for its varied climbing, hiking and mountain biking opportunities, Castle Rocks State Park is set among a stunning backdrop of rugged mountains. The park’s unique campsites are located in Idaho’s largest pinyon pine forest in the shadow of 7,500-foot Smoky Mountain. The campground offers 37 sites for RV and tent campers, six equestrian sites and two rental yurts. On-site campground amenities include showers, flush toilets, electricity and a dump station.
